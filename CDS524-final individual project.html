Customer Churn Prediction — Hybrid Stacking Ensemble
Group Project
Jan 2026 — Mar 2026  |  Lingnan University
Problem
Customer churn is a core revenue risk in telecommunications. The team built a full end-to-end ML pipeline on the IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset (7,043 records, 21 variables, 26.54% churn rate) to identify which customers are likely to leave — enabling proactive, cost-effective retention campaigns with a positive business case.
Data & Stack
IBM Telco benchmark dataset. Preprocessing: stratified 80/20 split, iterative imputation, adaptive encoding (one-hot + target encoding), robust/standard/min-max scaling. Feature engineering: avg_monthly_spend, charge_ratio, tenure lifecycle flags, service-contract interaction features. Boruta feature selection + interaction-only polynomial features.
Approach
Hybrid stacking ensemble: XGBoost, CatBoost, LightGBM, Logistic Regression, Extra Trees, TabPFN as base experts. Out-of-fold stacking with sigmoid calibration and logistic regression meta-learner. Optuna hyperparameter optimization (50 trials per boosting model). Threshold optimized at 0.31. SHAP and LIME for global and local explainability.
Outcome & Impact
Held-out test set: ROC-AUC 0.847 · Recall 0.746 · F1 0.633 · Accuracy 0.770 · MCC 0.483. Correctly identified 279 churners (95 missed). Under project assumptions ($100 retention cost, $2,000 CLV, 50% success rate): estimated net benefit $38,200 · ROI 75.20%. Managerial recommendation: tiered retention workflow — prioritize month-to-month, electronic-check, and fiber-optic high-risk segments.
My Contribution: Led model evaluation, business interpretation, and report writing. Translated model outputs into business-facing net benefit and ROI estimates. Authored threshold comparison analysis, managerial retention strategy recommendations (segment-level action plans for month-to-month, electronic-check, and fiber-optic segments), SHAP/LIME explainability discussion, and full report documentation. Co-reviewed all results for consistency with the code repository.

Maze Solver — Q-Learning Reinforcement Learning Agent
Individual Project
Feb 2026 — Mar 2026  |  Lingnan University
Problem
Designed and implemented a complete Q-learning RL agent to navigate a custom 8×8 grid-world maze with multiple interactive constraints: static walls, static and moving traps, collectible keys, and locked doors. The agent must learn a prerequisite sequence (collect key before opening door) and avoid dynamic hazards — demonstrating core RL principles in a multi-constraint, reward-sparse environment.
Environment Design
Custom maze: 58 discrete states, 6 static traps, 2 moving traps (dynamic trajectories), 2 collectible keys, 2 locked doors, treasure goal at (7,7). Reward shaping: +100 treasure, +20 key, −50 trap, −10 door (no key), −3 revisit penalty (anti-looping). Libraries: NumPy (Q-table), PyGame (rendering), Matplotlib (learning curves, Q-table heatmap).
Approach
Q-learning with epsilon-greedy exploration: α=0.1, γ=0.95, ε decayed 1.0→0.01 (rate 0.995) over 2,000 training episodes. Three operational modes: Training Mode (live viz, 30 FPS, epsilon tracking), Player Mode (arrow-key human play), Demo Mode (5 FPS optimal path replay). Moving trap positions encoded in state representation to handle dynamic hazards.
Outcome & Impact
Agent achieved >92% success rate post-training. Three clear learning phases: Phase 1 (ep. 1–600) random exploration, avg. reward −120 to −50, <10% success; Phase 2 (ep. 600–1,400) skill acquisition, 10%→70% success; Phase 3 (ep. 1,400–2,000) convergence, avg. reward +87, 92% success. Learned 22-step optimal path — correctly sequences key collection before door passage and avoids all static and moving traps.
Full Individual Ownership: End-to-end solo implementation — custom maze environment class (EnhancedMazeEnv), Q-table architecture, reward shaping logic, epsilon-greedy policy, multi-mode interactive interface (PyGame), training visualization pipeline (Matplotlib learning curves and Q-table heatmap), and all challenge solutions (revisit penalty for anti-looping, moving trap state tracking, phased exploration schedule). Full report and code authored independently.

Contemporary College Students' Online Luxury Goods Purchasing Behavior
Individual Project
Sep 2023 — May 2024  |  Shenzhen University
Problem
Understanding what drives contemporary college students to purchase luxury goods online — a consumer segment growing rapidly in China yet understudied quantitatively. The research aimed to identify key behavioral drivers and provide empirical evidence for both luxury brand marketers and consumer education policymakers.
Data
Primary survey data — 260 valid questionnaires collected online from contemporary college students across China. Variables covered consumption attitude, purchase motivation (hedonic, status, self-expression), social influence, platform trust, and actual purchase behavior.
Approach
Full quantitative pipeline in SPSS: reliability analysis (Cronbach's α), validity testing (EFA/CFA), Pearson correlation analysis, and multiple regression analysis. Theoretical framework grounded in consumer behavior theory, Maslow's hierarchy, and TAM (Technology Acceptance Model).
Outcome & Impact
Validated that consumption attitude, social identity motivation, and platform trust are significant predictors of online luxury purchase intent. Results provide evidence-based recommendations for luxury brands' digital marketing strategies and rational consumption education. Full thesis accepted by Shenzhen University.
Full Individual Ownership: End-to-end solo project — literature review, theoretical model construction, questionnaire design, online survey distribution and collection, all SPSS statistical analyses, result interpretation, and academic writing. Supervised by Prof. Li Tong (Professor, School of Management, Shenzhen University).
